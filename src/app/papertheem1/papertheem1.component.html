Board of Secondary Education

Chemistry Examination - 9th Class

Duration: 3 Hours | Total Marks: 75

Section A: Short Answer Questions

You are required to respond to all of the following briefly. Be concise and clear.

Provide concise answers:

  • Define a chemical reaction. Provide an example.
  • Explain how elements are arranged in the periodic table and its importance.
  • What are isotopes? Give examples and their significance.
  • Describe electrolysis and its industrial uses.
  • Differentiate between an atom and a molecule with diagrams.
  • Discuss the three states of matter with everyday examples.
  • State and explain the Law of Conservation of Mass.
  • What is a catalyst and how does it affect reactions?
  • Explain valency and how it is determined.

Continue with the following:

  • Define acids and bases according to the Arrhenius theory. Give examples.
  • Explain the pH scale and its importance in everyday life.
  • What is a buffer solution? Discuss its applications in chemistry.
  • Describe an oxidation reaction and its role in chemical processes.
  • Explain who the noble gases are and their properties.
  • Define chemical equilibrium and provide a practical example.
  • Explain Boyle’s Law and its significance in gases.
  • Differentiate between physical and chemical changes with examples.
  • Calculate the molar mass of water (H₂O).

Answer the following briefly:

  • Explain the atomic mass unit (amu) and its significance.
  • What are polymers? Provide examples and their uses in daily life.
  • Describe the process of electroplating and its industrial uses.
  • Define alkanes and give examples with their formula.
  • Discuss the kinetic theory of gases and its implications.
  • Differentiate between ionic and covalent bonds with diagrams.
  • What is an endothermic reaction? Provide an example.
  • Define Avogadro’s law and its applications.
  • What are allotropes? Give examples and their properties.

Section B: Detailed Questions

Attempt any two of the following. Each question contains two parts.

Provide detailed explanations:

a) Discuss the atomic structure, including protons, neutrons, and electrons.

b) Explain the atomic number and mass number and their importance in identifying an element.

Answer the following thoroughly:

a) Define chemical bonding and explain its types with examples.

b) Differentiate between ionic and covalent bonds using diagrams.

Consider the following and explain:

a) Explain electrolysis and its industrial applications.

b) Describe the process of electroplating and its significance in modern industries.
